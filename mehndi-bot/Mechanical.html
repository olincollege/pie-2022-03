MECHANICAL

XY Gantry

The XY gantry uses three stepper motors, timing belts, and timing pulleys that are mounted using 3D-printed parts. We connected each motor shaft to a timing pulley with a corresponding idler timing pulley attached to the other end of the aluminum framing extrusion using an axle. We then added A timing belt to each setup such that it aligns with the pulleys and goes into the outside tracks of the aluminum framing extrusion. 

The two ends of the belt go into the rectangular holes in the horizontal blue and black acrylic plates (y-axis) and yellow vertical plate (x-axis) and are secured using zip ties to control the belt's tension. Each plate slides on the side tracks of the aluminum framing extrusion using 4 V-Wheels set up such that they offset the plate 0.2in above the framing. The blue plate supports the idler pulley end of the aluminum framing extrusion for the x-axis, while the black plate supports the motor and timing pulley end.

Speed and Precision: Each timing and idler pulley has 20 teeth at a 2 mm pitch. The timing belt has a pitch of 2 mm. When the stepper motor shaft rotates, it allows for precise movement and speed because each full rotation moves the center framing and yellow plate 4 cm.

Z-Axis

The Z axis uses a rack and pinion mechanism to vertically move the syringe/pen. The yellow 3D-printed plate includes a mount for the servo that controls the vertical movement and holds the pinion. The plate also has an extrusion that acts as a sliding guide for the rack. It also sets a lower limit for the sliding such that the syringe/pen stays a minimum of 0.5 inches off the base of the gantry. This distance can be increased depending on the size of the hand under the syringe/pen. The rack includes the holder for the extrusion mechanism that moves vertically as the servo rotates the pinion.

Mehndi Extrusion Mechanism

For the Mehndi extrusion mechanism, we had to design a lightweight way to contract a syringe that was also compact enough to attach to our z-axis gantry easily. We settled on a simple but efficient approach where the geared DC motor would attach to the side of the extruder with friction clips. Then a cord of fish line would be attached between the top of the extruder and the motor shaft to facilitate the actual extrusion, as an early mockup below illustrates.

This design was ultimately successful but did have some teething issues. Specifically, the early design’s friction clips were not sturdy enough and broke when the motor pulled down with too much force. In addition, the retention clip on the top of the syringe that held the fishing line misaligned the pulling force, so it was unevenly distributed, causing the mechanism to jam. A second revision pictured below fixed all these issues, providing a lightweight and effective mechanism.

This design interops with the z-axis rack and pinwheel system to form the overall mechanism. In addition to the extrusion itself, we added a silicone tip to the syringe to ensure the even distribution of mehndi paste and to prevent the mechanism from harming the user's hand.

Camera Mount

For the camera to recognize the size and shape of a user’s hand, it needs to be positioned about 15 inches above the hand. To accomplish this, we used laser-cut hardwood as the horizontal camera mount and an aluminum framing extrusion as the vertical component.

CIRCUIT BOX

With multiple electrical components spread around the gantry, we needed a circuit box to manage wires and give our design a cleaner look. We laser-cut the six faces of our box and assembled them using comb joints.

Design DecisionS

Belt and Sprocket for XY

Rack and Pinion for Z

We decided on a belt and sprocket mechanism for our final XY gantry after experimenting with multiple gantry mechanisms like lead screw and slider and rack and pinion. We discovered the belt and sprocket to be the most precise and quick compared to the other two. It also is the most stable and can support the weight of the extrusion mechanism without failing.

After experimenting with rack and pinion for the XY gantry, we found it helpful when there is a constrained movement. Since we only needed our syringe/pen to lift/drop a set distance vertically, we chose the rack and pinion mechanism. It also supports the extrusion mechanism and can easily move with its weight on the rack.
