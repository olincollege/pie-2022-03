ELECTRICAL

GANTRY motors

In order to manipulate the belts on the XY gantry, we use NEMA17 12V stepper motors as they are advantageous for speed control, positioning precision, movement repeatability, and reliabiity. This is especially important because mehndi designs are often very intricate and contain many curves. Stepper motors which high steps count would allow the robot to produce continuous. To help interface with the motors, we are using ​A4988 drivers. In order to reduce inconsistencies in motor movement, we connected a 47µF capacitor to each of our motor.

POWER SUPPLY

Each stepper motor needs a constant power supply of 12V 2A, luckily, we were able to find a free power supply that provides the exact amount. However, the power supply that we have is rather old and we ran into a lot of issues integrating this power supply with our system. Adding cable management helped ensure we can use the power supply in a safe manner.

EXTRUSION

The extrusion mechanism presented numerous electrical challenges stemming from the fact it had to move with the Z-axis mechanism. Namely, we had to implement long flexible wires that resisted tangling, a H bridge circuit to control the speed and direction of the motor, and a linear voltage regulator to step down the input voltage to a consistent 5V supply. In addition to these, the DC motor used to pull down the syringe had to be strong enough to overcome the static friction of the paste. Fortunately, there were geared DC motors on hand that had more than enough mechanical advantage, and we were also able to solder a proto breadboard with all of the necessary motor control components as seen in the figure on the right. 

cable management

Between the camera and the Arduino that are connected to the laptop, the power supply and the 3 stepper motors, our wiring system is quite complicated. The Electrical Subteam had to work closely with the Mechanical Subteam to ensure that the wiring placement is clean and does not interfere with the gantry movements.
