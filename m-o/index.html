M-O

A remote controlled recreation of the robot M-O from the movie WALL-E

Overall Goal

Create a robot which resembles and moves around like M-O from WALL-E .

MVP

  • Resemble M-O
  • Fully functional remote control
  • Functional LED screen with eyes

Stretch Goals

  • Movement - Arms, Head L & R, Head Up & Down
  • Emoted animations
  • Speaker for M-O noises
  • Collision prevention - lights/screen affected by proximity to objects

*Highlighted sections in the strech goals were completed successfully.

Sprint 1 - "Box That Moves"

For the first sprint, we used a cardboard box to determine the final sizing for M-O. We also put wheels on said cardboard box and tested wireless joystick-controlled driving.

Sprint 2 - "Fancier Box That Moves"

For the second sprint, we used a laser-cut box to make a new version of M-O, this time with a head and arms. We also successfully installed a reachargable battery onto the main body of M-O. The 16x32 RGB Matrix also arrived and we were able to get basic blinking eyes on M-O's face.

Sprint 3 - "It's a M-O"

For the third sprint, we made our laser-box more skeleton-like in order to prepare for the future shell going onto it. A new head and neck were made in order to support movement, and servos were added to mount the arms onto. The Matrix for the eyes were animated for two different expressions, and a physical iteration of the remote controller was completed.

Demo - "Holy S-M-O-Kes"

For the demo, we made the shell for our skeleton M-O and painted him white as well. We also made new, rounder arms as well as a backpack and proper roller to enhance his appearance. A lot of the software library integration with radio chips was also finalized.
